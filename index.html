Academic Programs

The University of Michigan offers a diverse array of academic programs, with over 250 undergraduate majors spanning various fields, including engineering, business, the arts, and the sciences. The curriculum is designed to provide a rigorous education, with clear course requirements that emphasize both foundational knowledge and experiential learning. Faculty members are renowned experts in their fields, committed to student success and research innovation. With a strong focus on interdisciplinary studies, UMich fosters an environment where students can explore their passions and prepare for future careers.

Student Life

At the University of Michigan, student life is vibrant and engaging, supported by state-of-the-art campus facilities and amenities. With over 1,600 student organizations and clubs, theres something for everyone, from academic societies to cultural groups. Social and extracurricular activities abound, including events, concerts, and volunteer opportunities that foster community and connection.
Housing options range from residence halls to apartment-style living, with costs varying to accommodate different budgets. UMich’s dynamic campus ensures students have a well-rounded experience both inside and outside the classroom.

Cost & Financial Aid

The University of Michigan offers a comprehensive overview of costs and financial aid for prospective students. Tuition and fees vary based on residency status, with in-state students enjoying a lower rate compared to out-of-state students. Financial aid options are robust, including scholarships, grants, and loans to help make education more accessible. Students can explore various payment plans to manage their expenses effectively, with deadlines that ensure timely processing of aid applications. With dedicated resources, UMich is committed to supporting students in navigating their financial journey.

Alumni Success

Gerald Ford: 38th President of the United States and former Football player who played for the University of Michigan. He graduated with a degree in economics in 1935.

Larry Page: Co-founder of google and wealthiest graduate from the University of Michigan. Net worth with $146.1 billion & he graduated in 1995.

Tom Brady: Seven-time Super Bowl champion and renowned. His net worth is $333 million, and he graduated from the University of Michigan in 1999.

Madonna: superstar and cultural icon known for her music and influential career. With a net worth of $850 million, she graduated from the University of Michigan in 1978.

The University of Michigan has strong employment rates for its graduates, with high percentages of graduates finding jobs and earning good salaries:

The Ross Business School: The 2022 Full-Time MBA class had a record-breaking year with 99% of graduates receiving job offers within three months of graduation, and 98.4% accepting those offers. University of Michigan in Ann Arbor: 94% of graduates are employed two years after graduation, and 92% are employed six years after graduation.
